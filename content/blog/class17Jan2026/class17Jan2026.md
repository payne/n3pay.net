The formula for converting frequency to wavelength is based on the speed of radio waves, which travel at the speed of light (approximately 300,000,000 meters per second),. To simplify the math for radio work, you can divide the speed of light by the frequency.

For practical calculations using Megahertz (MHz), the formula is:

$$ \text{Wavelength (in meters)} = \frac{300}{\text{Frequency (in MHz)}} $$

Conversely, if you know the wavelength, you can determine the frequency using:

$$ \text{Frequency (in MHz)} = \frac{300}{\text{Wavelength (in meters)}} $$

**Relationship to Amateur Bands**
Amateur radio bands are often identified by their approximate wavelength rather than their specific frequency range. The formula demonstrates the inverse relationship between the two: as the frequency gets higher, the wavelength gets shorter.

*   **2 Meter Band:** This band spans 144 MHz to 148 MHz. If you calculate the wavelength for 148 MHz ($300 / 148$), the result is approximately 2.03 meters, which is why it is referred to as the 2-meter band.
*   **6 Meter Band:** This band begins at 50 MHz. Using the formula ($300 / 50$), the result is exactly 6 meters.
*   **70 Centimeter Band:** This band is in the UHF range (around 440 MHz). Calculating $300 / 440$ yields approximately 0.68 meters, or 68 centimeters (commonly referred to as the 70 cm band).
