# Amateur Radio Nets on 2 Meters in Omaha, Nebraska

The Omaha metropolitan area hosts an active amateur radio community with numerous 2-meter nets serving the region throughout the week. **Douglas County's primary emergency repeater K0USA (146.940 MHz) anchors the most significant nets**, while several other repeaters provide comprehensive coverage across the metro area including Council Bluffs, Iowa. The region's amateur radio infrastructure supports both emergency communications through ARES and casual ham radio activities across multiple clubs.

Most nets operate in Central Time with excellent coverage throughout the Omaha metro area, extending into eastern Nebraska and western Iowa. The **Douglas County ARES net on Sunday evenings serves as the primary emergency communications training net** for the region, while numerous club nets provide opportunities for technical discussion, casual conversation, and specialized interests throughout the week.

## Emergency and seasonal nets

**Douglas County SKYWARN Net**
- **Primary Frequency:** 146.940 MHz (-) K0USA repeater
- **Backup Frequencies:** 147.360, 145.450, 147.390 MHz  
- **Activation:** During severe weather warnings
- **Purpose:** Severe weather spotting and reporting to National Weather Service
- **Coverage:** Douglas County and surrounding areas
- **Notes:** Live audio feed available on Broadcastify during activation

**Lancaster County SKYWARN** 
- **Primary Frequency:** 146.760 MHz (-) K0KKV repeater
- **Backup Frequencies:** 147.240, 146.850, 147.045 MHz
- **Activation:** During severe weather warnings in Lincoln area
- **Purpose:** Weather spotting for Lancaster County/Lincoln

## Key repeater information

**Major 2-Meter Repeaters Serving Omaha Metro:**

- **K0USA 146.940 MHz (-)** - Ak-Sar-Ben ARC, PL 131.8 Hz output only, primary Douglas County ARES
- **K0SWI 146.820 MHz (-)** - Southwest Iowa ARC, PL 136.5 Hz, Council Bluffs
- **N0WKF 145.290 MHz (-)** - Heartland Hams, Glenwood, IA
- **W0WYV 147.390 MHz (+)** - Bellevue ARC, PL 131.8 Hz  
- **K0BOY 147.360 MHz (+)** - Omaha area
- **K0ASH 145.310 MHz (-)** - Ashland ARC
- **KD0EFC 146.670 MHz (-)** - Fremont area, PL 100.0 Hz
- **K0KKV 146.760 MHz (-)** - Lincoln ARC, eastern Nebraska coverage

## Important notes

**Time Zone:** All times listed are Central Time (CDT/CST). **Verification:** Net schedules may change periodically - operators should verify current information with local clubs or net control stations. **Coverage:** Most repeaters provide excellent mobile and portable coverage throughout the Omaha metropolitan area, extending into Council Bluffs, Iowa and surrounding counties. **Emergency Priority:** During severe weather or emergency situations, scheduled nets may be suspended to allow emergency traffic priority on repeaters.

**Primary Contacts:** Douglas County ARES coordination through Metro Douglas ARES (mdares.org), Nebraska ARES section through K0RPT, and major club information available through Ak-Sar-Ben Amateur Radio Club (aksarbenarc.org) and Southwest Iowa Amateur Radio Club (swiradio.org).
